4.2. Duress Procedures.


4.2.1. (DCNI) The SCS will act as the SEDS Operator. The responsibility of the SEDS Operator is to act as part of the 2-person concept in activating RTES. In the event the RTES operator performs an unauthorized function while the system is active, the SCS has system disable control via the E-Stop button/O- Stop buttons. The system will remain disabled until the E-Stop/O-Stop is returned to its normal state.


4.2.2. (DCNI) Duress in the SSCC: If there is duress in the SSCC or the RTES operator is performing an unauthorized action in coordination with the SCS, the KUMMSC Entry Controller has system disable control via the RDDS. The RTES will be

disabled until the RDDS is returned to its normal state. NOTE: The RTES may need to be rebooted since the RDDS removes all electrical power to the remote electronics.
